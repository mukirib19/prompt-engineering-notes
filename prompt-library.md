# Prompt library

A collection of 8 tested prompt templates for content writing and strategy tasks. Each prompt is written out in full and ready to use or adapt. The examples use a fictional AI productivity company called Zara AI, based in Nairobi, targeting small business owners and freelancers in East Africa.

---

## Prompt 1 — Blog post generation

**Purpose:**
To generate a complete, well-structured first draft of a blog post on a given topic, written for a specific audience and in a defined tone.

**Prompt:**
```
You are an experienced content writer. Write a blog post of approximately 700 words on the topic of how small business owners in East Africa can use AI tools to save time on administrative tasks.

The target audience is small business owners and freelancers in Kenya, Uganda, and Tanzania who are curious about AI but have no technical background. The tone should be conversational, informative, and practical. Avoid jargon. Do not use filler phrases like "in today's fast-paced world" or "it is important to note."

Structure the post with:
- A direct, engaging opening that states what the reader will get from the article
- Three to four main sections, each with a clear subheading
- A conclusion that summarises the key point and gives the reader one actionable next step

Do not include an introduction that restates the title. Begin with substance.
```

**When to use it:**
Use this prompt when you need a first draft quickly and have a clear topic, audience, and tone in mind. It works well for informational and how-to blog posts. Review the output for factual accuracy and adjust the voice as needed before publishing.

## Prompt 2 — AI output evaluation

**Purpose:**
To systematically evaluate an AI-generated piece of content across the key quality dimensions: accuracy, clarity, tone, structure, and relevance.

**Prompt:**
```
You are a senior content editor with expertise in evaluating AI-generated content. I am going to give you a piece of content. Evaluate it across the following five dimensions and provide a written assessment for each one:

1. Factual accuracy — are all claims credible and verifiable? Flag any statements that appear incorrect, unsupported, or that require verification.
2. Clarity — is the writing easy to understand? Identify any sentences or sections that are unnecessarily complex or unclear.
3. Tone — does the tone suit the intended audience and purpose? Note any inconsistencies or sections where the tone shifts without reason.
4. Structure — does the content follow a logical structure? Assess whether each section earns its place and whether the argument or information flow makes sense.
5. Relevance — is the content specific and useful, or does it feel generic? Flag any sections that could apply to any brand or audience without modification.

For each dimension, give a rating of 1 to 5 and a brief written explanation. End with an overall assessment and your top three recommendations for improvement.

Here is the content to evaluate:

---

AI tools are changing the way businesses operate. More and more companies are using artificial intelligence to automate tasks, improve efficiency, and reduce costs. It is important to note that AI is not just for large corporations — small businesses can benefit too. In today's fast-paced world, staying ahead of the competition means embracing new technologies. AI tools can help with customer service, marketing, data analysis, and much more. As we have seen, the possibilities are endless.

---
```

**When to use it:**
Use this prompt when reviewing AI-generated content before publication, or when working on AI evaluation tasks for platforms that require structured quality assessment. The sample content above is intentionally weak — it demonstrates exactly the kind of generic, filler-heavy writing this evaluation process is designed to catch.

## Prompt 3 — Human tone rewriting

**Purpose:**
To rewrite AI-generated or overly formal content so that it reads like it was written by a real person, with a natural voice and no generic filler.

**Prompt:**
```
Rewrite the following content so that it sounds like it was written by a knowledgeable human being, not by an AI.

Apply these rules:
- Use plain, direct language. Say things once, clearly.
- Remove all filler phrases such as "it is worth noting," "in conclusion," "as we have seen," and "it goes without saying."
- Vary sentence length. Mix short, punchy sentences with longer ones where needed for flow.
- Do not start sentences with "I" repeatedly. Vary the structure.
- Keep all the information intact — do not cut facts or change the meaning. Only change the language and tone.
- The final result should feel like something a confident, experienced writer produced — not a system generating text.

Here is the content to rewrite:

---

Zara AI is a revolutionary platform that is changing the way small businesses operate in East Africa. It is worth noting that our cutting-edge AI technology provides world-class solutions for entrepreneurs who are looking to streamline their operations. As we have seen, businesses that embrace AI are better positioned for success. In conclusion, Zara AI is the best choice for any business owner who wants to stay ahead of the competition and achieve their goals in today's dynamic business environment.

---
```

**When to use it:**
Use this prompt on any AI output that feels stiff, overly formal, or generic. It is particularly useful for brand copy, blog posts, and any client-facing content where voice and personality matter. The sample above is a typical example of AI copy that sounds promotional but says very little — exactly what this rewrite process is designed to fix.

## Prompt 4 — Content calendar creation

**Purpose:**
To generate a structured monthly content calendar for a brand, covering multiple platforms and content formats, based on a defined theme or strategic focus.

**Prompt:**
```
You are a content strategist. Create a four-week content calendar for Zara AI, a Nairobi-based software company that makes AI productivity tools for small business owners and freelancers in East Africa.

Details:
- Industry: AI and technology software
- Target audience: Small business owners and freelancers in Kenya, Uganda, and Tanzania, aged 25 to 45, who want to work more efficiently but are not technical
- Content goal for the month: Educate the audience about practical AI use cases and drive sign-ups for a free trial of the Zara AI platform
- Platforms to cover: LinkedIn, Instagram, company blog, and weekly email newsletter
- Posting frequency: Five posts per week on LinkedIn, four posts per week on Instagram, two blog posts per month, one newsletter per week

For each piece of content, provide:
- The week and day
- The platform
- The content format (e.g. article, carousel, short video script, email)
- The topic or title
- A one-sentence description of the angle or key message

Present the calendar in a table format with columns for: week, day, platform, format, topic, and key message.
```

**When to use it:**
Use this prompt at the start of a new month or campaign planning cycle. It works best when strategic priorities are already defined and you need a structured execution plan. Always review the output and adjust it to reflect any brand-specific constraints, upcoming events, or timely opportunities in your market.

## Prompt 5 — Technical document summarising

**Purpose:**
To summarise a long or complex technical document into a clear, accessible overview that a non-specialist audience can understand and act on.

**Prompt:**
```
You are a technical writer with experience making complex information accessible to general audiences. I am going to give you a technical document. Summarise it according to the following instructions:

1. Write a two to three sentence overview of what the document is about and why it matters.
2. List the five to seven most important points from the document. For each point, write two to three sentences in plain language. Avoid technical jargon unless you also explain it.
3. If the document includes any recommendations, action items, or conclusions, summarise them clearly under a separate heading called "Key takeaways."
4. If there are any terms, acronyms, or concepts that a general reader is unlikely to know, include a brief glossary at the end.

The summary should be readable by someone with no specialist knowledge of the subject. Do not include your own opinions or analysis — only what is in the document.

Here is the document to summarise:

---

Zara AI Platform — Technical Integration Guide v2.1

This document outlines the API integration process for third-party developers connecting to the Zara AI platform. The RESTful API uses OAuth 2.0 for authentication. All requests must include a bearer token in the Authorization header. Rate limiting is enforced at 1,000 requests per hour per API key. Data is returned in JSON format. Webhook support is available for real-time event notifications. Developers should refer to the endpoint reference in Appendix B for a full list of available routes. Error handling follows standard HTTP status codes. Latency SLAs are documented in Section 4.3.

---
```

**When to use it:**
Use this prompt when you need to make technical content accessible for a broader audience — for example, when summarising a product specification, integration guide, internal technical report, or research paper for a non-technical team, client, or stakeholder.

## Prompt 6 — Article outline generation

**Purpose:**
To generate a detailed, logical article outline before writing a full draft, ensuring the structure is sound before any writing begins.

**Prompt:**
```
You are an experienced content strategist and editor. Generate a detailed outline for an article on the following topic:

Topic: How freelancers in East Africa can use AI tools to win more clients and deliver better work
Target audience: Freelancers in Kenya, Uganda, and Tanzania who are familiar with platforms like Fiverr and Upwork but have not yet explored AI tools
Intended word count: 900 words
Tone: Practical, direct, and encouraging — written by someone who understands the freelance market in the region, not a generic global audience
Goal of the article: Educate freelancers about three to four specific AI tools or use cases that can improve their work quality and increase their income

The outline should include:
- A working title
- A brief note on the angle or central argument of the article (two to three sentences)
- An introduction section with a note on what it should accomplish
- Four to six main sections, each with:
  - A suggested subheading
  - Three to five bullet points describing what that section should cover
- A conclusion section with a note on what it should accomplish and whether it should include a call to action

Do not write the full article — only the outline. Make the bullet points specific enough that a writer could work from them without further instruction.
```

**When to use it:**
Use this prompt before writing any long-form content. Reviewing and approving an outline before drafting saves significant editing time and ensures the final piece has a logical structure. It is especially useful for articles over 800 words or on topics with multiple possible angles.

## Prompt 7 — Product description writing

**Purpose:**
To write a compelling, accurate product description that communicates key features and benefits clearly and in a tone that matches the brand.

**Prompt:**
```
You are a copywriter specialising in product descriptions. Write a product description for the following product.

Product name: Zara AI Scheduler
Product category: AI productivity tool for small businesses
Key features: Automatic meeting scheduling, smart calendar conflict detection, integration with Google Calendar and Outlook, WhatsApp reminder notifications, available in English and Swahili
Key benefits: Saves two to three hours per week on scheduling admin, eliminates double bookings, keeps clients and team members informed automatically, works on mobile without needing a desktop setup
Target customer: Small business owners and freelancers in East Africa who manage their own schedules and communicate heavily through WhatsApp and email. They are time-poor, practical, and skeptical of tools that overcomplicate simple tasks.
Tone: Warm, direct, and practical — like advice from a trusted colleague, not a sales pitch
Length: 130 words

Instructions:
- Lead with the most compelling benefit, not a feature list.
- Use the second person — address the customer directly as "you."
- Do not use superlatives like "best," "revolutionary," or "world-class."
- End with a clear, low-pressure call to action.

Write one version only.
```

**When to use it:**
Use this prompt for product pages, app store listings, service descriptions, or pitch decks where a product needs to be described concisely and persuasively. Adjust the length and tone to match the platform and the audience's level of familiarity with the product category.

## Prompt 8 — Email newsletter drafting

**Purpose:**
To draft a complete email newsletter for a brand or individual, covering a defined topic, in a consistent voice, with a clear structure and call to action.

**Prompt:**
```
You are an email copywriter. Draft a newsletter email for the following:

Sender name: Benard Mukiri Maina, writing as the Zara AI content team
Audience: Small business owners and freelancers in East Africa who subscribed to the Zara AI newsletter to learn practical tips on using AI in their work
Topic of this edition: Three AI tools that save freelancers time on client communication
Key message: Good client communication does not have to take hours — the right AI tools can handle the routine parts so you can focus on the work itself
Tone: Conversational, practical, and direct — like a knowledgeable friend sharing useful advice, not a brand broadcasting marketing content
Call to action: Click through to read the full article on the Zara AI blog
Approximate length: 300 words

Structure the email as follows:
- Subject line (provide two options)
- Preview text (one sentence, under 90 characters)
- Opening — one to two sentences that draw the reader in without being clickbait
- Body — the main content, covering the topic clearly and in the defined tone
- Call to action — clear, specific, and low-pressure
- Sign-off — warm and personal, consistent with the sender's voice

Do not use generic newsletter openers like "I hope this finds you well" or "Welcome to this week's edition." Start with something the reader will find immediately useful or interesting.
```

**When to use it:**
Use this prompt for regular newsletter editions, product announcements, or onboarding email sequences. It works best when you have a clear topic and a well-defined audience. Always read through the output and adjust the voice and any timely details to reflect your actual tone and context before sending.
